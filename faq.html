Frequently Asked Questions

What does "scratching" mean?

In billiards, "scratching" refers to the act of sinking the cue ball (white ball) into a pocket. This is generally considered a penalty shot and results in the opponent receiving "ball in hand," meaning they can place the cue ball anywhere on the table for their next shot.

What is the difference between "English" and "side spin"?

"English" and "side spin" are terms used to describe the spin applied to the cue ball when striking it. "English" refers to the spin applied to the cue ball in a horizontal direction, while "side spin" refers to the spin applied in a vertical direction. Both types of spin can affect the trajectory and behavior of the cue ball, allowing players to control their shots more effectively.

What is the "break" in billiards?

The "break" is the opening shot of a billiards game, where the player strikes the cue ball to scatter the racked balls on the table. The break is an important shot as it can set the tone for the rest of the game and can potentially lead to a significant advantage for the player who executes it well.

What is the "bank shot" in billiards?

A "bank shot" is a type of shot in billiards where the player strikes the cue ball to hit one or more cushions (the sides of the table) before hitting the target ball. Bank shots can be used to navigate around obstacles on the table or to create more difficult shots for opponents.
