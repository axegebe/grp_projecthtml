Regulations

1. Always respect your opponent and the game.
2. No foul language or unsportsmanlike conduct will be tolerated.
3. Players must wait for their turn and not interfere with their opponent's shots.
4. The use of any performance-enhancing substances is strictly prohibited.
5. Players must adhere to the official rules of billiards as set by the governing body.

Failure to comply with these rules may result in penalties, including disqualification from the game or tournament.

Game Rules

Objective: The main objective of billiards is to use the cue stick to strike the cue ball and sink the object balls into the designated pockets according to the rules of the specific game variant.

Game Setup: The table is set up with 15 numbered balls (for pool) or 22 balls (for snooker). The cue ball (white) is placed at the head of the table, and players take turns striking it to hit other balls.

Legal Shots: A shot is considered legal if the cue ball hits one of the player's assigned balls first. The player must sink at least one ball in a pocket on their first shot to establish their group (solids or stripes in pool).

Fouls: Common fouls include not hitting your assigned ball first, failing to hit any rail, jumping the cue ball off the table, and moving other balls with the cue stick. A foul results in the opponent getting ball-in-hand.

Winning: The player who successfully pockets all their assigned balls and then sinks the 8-ball (in pool) or wins by points (in snooker) wins the game.
